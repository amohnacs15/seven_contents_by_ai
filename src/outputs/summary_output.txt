Aged care workers have a higher than average risk of suffering physical injury while on the job. These injuries are often caused by improper practice of manual handling, as the unique environment of residential aged care can create risks and hazards that do not appear in other professional settings. To stay safe, workers must have an understanding of both the risks and rewards of the care they are providing, so that the specific risks are managed properly. All workers must be proactive in preventing unhealthy physical hazards and difficulties in behaviors, and the topics covered in this course include manual tasks, behavior management, and infection prevention. Such attention and understanding of the potential risks can help ensure that accidents, illnesses, and injury are avoided in a secure and supportive workplace.
